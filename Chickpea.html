BBQ Chickpea Salad with Avocado

This fresh and colorful vegan BBQ Chickpea Salad is so flavorful & satisfying! It's tossed with a homemade avocado ranch dressing, then topped with warm, saucy BBQ chickpeas. (Vegan, gluten-free, oil-free.)

Ingredients

For this recipe you'll toss together in a large bowl:

  • Garbanzo beans (chickpeas) - These are perfect for a plant-based chicken replacement. Chickpeas have a tender, hearty texture and are rich in plant-based protein and fiber. They're absolutely delicious simmered in BBQ sauce!.
  • BBQ sauce - Use your own favorite BBQ sauce for this recipe. Either homemade or store bought works great
  • Romaine lettuce - You'll need one large head romaine, or about 5-6 cups chopped. I like romaine best, but you can substitute other baby greens, kale, spinach, or lettuce instead.
  • Cooked sweet corn - You can either use cooked fresh, canned, frozen, or fire-roasted.
  • Grape tomatoes - Or cherry tomato, roma tomato, plum tomato, or other garden tomatoes. I like grape tomatoes best in this recipe because they have a rich, sweet flavor.
  • Shredded carrots - Either buy pre-shredded carrots in the produce section for convenience, or you can shred/grate your own..
  • Green onion (scallion) -Or you can use red onion for a stronger flavor..
  • Ranch dressing - It's made with ingredients like fresh herbs, vinegar, lemon juice, dairy-free milk, and creamy avocado.

Customizing

  • Veggies - Add other vegetables like cucumber, shredded red cabbage, sugar snap peas, bell pepper, diced avocado, or whatever else sounds good.
  • Fresh herbs - For even more flavor, include herbs like fresh parsley, cilantro, basil, dill, or chives to taste.

Storing

  • - Store the dressing, BBQ chickpeas, and salad in separate airtight containers in the refrigerator. (This helps it stay fresh & crisp.) Then toss it together when ready to serve. If stored separately, I find it keeps for about 3-4 days.

Instructions

  • Make the dressing -Or you can prepare this in advance and store in the fridge until ready.
  • Make the BBQ chickpeas - Rinse and drain chickpeas. In a skillet over medium-low heat, add chickpeas and BBQ sauce. Simmer for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. (Feel free to add a splash of vegetable broth or water to slightly thin the sauce if desired.).
  • Prepare salad - Meanwhile, chop the romaine, tomatoes, and green onion. Place in a bowl. Add corn and carrots. Toss to combine.
  • Assemble - When ready to assemble, toss salad with desired amount of dressing, then top with warm BBQ chickpeas.

The flavors come together so nicely, plus it's easy to customize with your favorite veggies. It's great for lunch, dinner, take-along lunches, meal prep, etc.
